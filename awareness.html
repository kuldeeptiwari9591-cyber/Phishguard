Understand Phishing Before It Tricks You

Most phishing attacks succeed not because of technology, but because of human trust, urgency, and confusion. This page helps you recognize those traps.

What Is Phishing?

Phishing is an attempt to trick you into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, banking details, or personal data. Attackers pretend to be trusted organizations like banks, payment apps, delivery companies, or government services.

These attacks usually create urgency, fear, or excitement so you act without verifying.

Common Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be from banks, Google, PayPal, or companies. They often claim your account is suspended or needs verification.

Spear Phishing

Targeted attacks where the attacker already knows your name, workplace, or role, making the message feel personal and legitimate.

Smishing (SMS)

Fraudulent text messages such as “Your package is delayed” or “Your bank account is blocked” with a short malicious link.

Vishing (Phone Calls)

Calls pretending to be bank officials or tech support, asking for OTPs or remote access to your device.

Clone Phishing

A legitimate email you previously received is copied, but the link or attachment is replaced with a malicious one.

Fake Websites

Websites that look identical to real login pages but are hosted on newly created or suspicious domains.

How to Stay Safe

  • Never enter passwords or OTPs from links in emails or messages
  • Check the website domain carefully before logging in
  • Be suspicious of urgency, threats, or unexpected rewards
  • Use official apps or manually type website addresses
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible
